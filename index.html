Global News Vocabulary
  • Escalate (verb): To increase rapidly; to intensify.
    Example: Tensions **escalated** quickly after the border incident.
  • Casualties (noun): Persons killed or injured in a war or accident.
    Example: The conflict resulted in a high number of **casualties**.
  • Mediate (verb): To intervene between people or groups in a dispute to bring about an agreement.
    Example: The UN offered to **mediate** the peace talks.
  • Humanitarian Crisis (noun phrase): A single event or a series of events that are threatening in terms of health, safety, or well-being of a community or large group of people.
    Example: The region is experiencing a severe **humanitarian crisis** due to lack of food and water.
  • Stall (verb): To stop making progress or developing.
    Example: Peace negotiations have **stalled** due to disagreements.
  • Blockade (noun): An act or means of sealing off a place to prevent goods or people from entering or leaving.
    Example: The **blockade** has severely restricted the flow of essential supplies.
  • Recall (verb): To officially order someone to return to a place, or to bring someone back from a political position.
    Example: Voters decided to **recall** the representative after public outcry.
  • Pro-China (adjective): Supporting or favoring China.
    Example: The **pro-China** faction pushed for closer economic ties.
Brazil & Trump Specific Vocabulary
  • Looming (adjective): Appearing as a shadowy form, especially one that is large or threatening; about to happen.
    Example: The **looming** economic recession worried investors.
  • Tariff (noun): A tax or duty to be paid on a particular class of imports or exports.
    Example: The government imposed a new **tariff** on imported steel.
  • Devastate (verb): To destroy or cause severe damage or suffering to.
    Example: The storm could **devastate** the coastal communities.
  • Coup Plot (noun phrase): A secret plan to overthrow a government.
    Example: Security services uncovered a **coup plot** against the president.
  • Witch-hunt (noun): A campaign directed against a person or group holding unpopular views.
    Example: He claimed the investigation was a political **witch-hunt**.
  • Scrutiny (noun): Critical observation or examination.
    Example: The company's accounts are under close **scrutiny**.
  • Ramifications (noun): A complex or unwelcome consequence of an action or event.
    Example: The new policy had unexpected **ramifications** for the entire industry.
  • Leverage (noun): Power to influence a person or situation.
    Example: She used her position to gain **leverage** in the negotiations.
  • Birthright Citizenship (noun phrase): A legal principle by which a person is granted citizenship of a country by virtue of having been born there.
    Example: The debate over **birthright citizenship** is a contentious issue.
