Key Vocabulary & Phrases

General Terms

  • Breakthrough: A significant discovery or development. (Ex: Scientists made a *breakthrough* in cancer research.)
  • Initiative: A new plan or process to achieve something. (Ex: The government launched a new *initiative* to reduce unemployment.)
  • Combat: To fight against or oppose. (Ex: They are working to *combat* poverty.)
  • Sustainable: Able to be maintained at a certain rate or level; causing little or no damage to the environment and therefore able to continue for a long time. (Ex: Renewable energy is a *sustainable* option.)
  • Pledge: A solemn promise or undertaking. (Ex: They *pledged* to donate a portion of their profits.)
  • Digestible: Easy to understand or absorb. (Ex: The complex information was presented in a *digestible* format.)

Business & Economy

  • Inflation: A general increase in prices and fall in the purchasing value of money. (Ex: High *inflation* can make everyday goods more expensive.)
  • Purchasing power: The financial ability to buy products and services. (Ex: Inflation reduces consumers' *purchasing power*.)
  • Hinder: To create difficulties for (someone or something), resulting in delay or obstruction. (Ex: Bad weather can *hinder* travel plans.)

Politics & Society

  • Unveil: To reveal or announce publicly for the first time. (Ex: The company will *unveil* its new product next month.)
  • Summit: A meeting between heads of government. (Ex: World leaders met at the G7 *summit*.)
